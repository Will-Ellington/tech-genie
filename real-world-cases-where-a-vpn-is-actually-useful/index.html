Real world cases where a VPN is actually useful

Will Ellington

VPNs are not a panacea for web security, but the internet world is defintiely divided between those who do and don't have a VPN. Below are some real world use cases where a VPN has helped prevent a serious problem.

  1. Protecting against cybercrime: A user was the victim of a cybercrime and had their personal information stolen. They started using a VPN to encrypt their internet traffic and protect their data from future attacks. The VPN also helped them access their bank account and other sensitive information securely.

  2. Working remotely: A user was working remotely and needed to access their company's network and files. By using a VPN, they were able to securely connect to their company's network and access the files they needed without compromising the security of the company's data.Â 

  3. Avoiding online price discrimination: A user noticed that they were being charged more for flights and hotel bookings when they searched from their home country. By using a VPN to connect to a server in a different country, they were able to avoid online price discrimination and save money on their bookings.

  4. Accessing online gaming servers: A user was unable to connect to online gaming servers due to geographical restrictions. By using a VPN to connect to a server in a different location, they were able to bypass the restrictions and connect to the gaming servers they needed.

  5. Protecting against workplace surveillance: A user was concerned that their employer was monitoring their internet activity while working remotely. By using a VPN, they were able to encrypt their internet traffic and prevent their employer from tracking their online activity.

  6. Protecting against cyberstalking: A user was being cyberstalked and wanted to protect their online activity from the stalker. By using a VPN, they were able to mask their IP address and prevent the stalker from tracking their online activity.
